The Life of Nellie Bly

  • 1864 - Born May 5 as Elizabeth Cochran in Pennsylvania.
  • 1879 - Elizabeth, aka "Pinky", enrolls at Indiana Normal School in hopes of becoming a teacher.
  • 1880- Elizabeth's mother moves the family to Philadelphia.
  • 1885 - Elizabeth reads an editorial in The Pennsylvania Dispatch titled "What Girls Are Good For" promting her to write a response to the paper. The editor, impressed by her writing, hires her under the pen name "Nellie Bly." Rather than writing typical "feminine" articles on gardening and fashion, Nellie wrote numerous articles exposing the trials of regular women in low status positions.
  • 1885 - Nellie travels to Mexico to be a foreign correspondent. She documents the imprisonment of a local journalist critical of the Mexican government and is forced to leave under threat of arrest.
  • 1887 - Nellie leaves The Dispatch in search of new opportunities in New York City. After four penniless months, she is hired by the New York World.
  • 1887 - Nellie takes an undercover assignment to document the terrible conditions at the Women's Lunatic Asylum on Blackwell's Island.
  • 1888 - Nellie suggests to her editor that she attempt a journey around the world inspiried by Jules Verne's Around the World in 80 Days.
  • 1889 - Nellie begins her 24,899-mile journey on November 14.
  • 1890 - On January 25, Nellie completes her 72-day journey; a world-record.
  • 1895 - Nellie marries millionaire manufacturer Robert Seaman, age 73 (Nellie is 31). She retires from journalism and becomes a partner in her husband's iron manufacturing business.
  • 1902 - Nellie invents files patents for an improved milk can and stacking garbage can.
  • 1904 - Nellie's husband dies and she becomes ownder of Iron Clad Manufacturing Co.
  • 1913 - Nellie covers the Women's Suffrage Parade and correctly predicts that women will gain the right to vote in 1920.
  • 1914 - Nellie's business goes bankrupt and she becomes America's first female war correspondent from the trenches of Europe.
  • 1919 - Nellie returns to American and becomes an advocate for social causes and poor children.
  • 1922 - Nellie dies in New York City.
